what_i_learned: |
  Today I learned more about feature engineering and its importance in improving machine learning model performance. I explored advanced techniques such as lagging and rolling features, which are commonly used in predictive analytics and time-series forecasting. Lagging allows a model to use information from previous time periods to help predict future outcomes. Rolling features, such as moving averages and rolling statistics, help smooth out short-term fluctuations and reveal underlying trends in the data. These techniques can provide a model with valuable historical context that would otherwise be difficult to capture.

  To conclude the day, I focused on feature selection as a method for further optimizing our prediction software. Feature selection helps identify the most relevant variables while removing unnecessary or redundant features that may introduce noise into the model. By reducing the number of inputs, models can often train faster and produce more reliable predictions. I learned that selecting the right features is just as important as creating new ones through feature engineering. Overall, today's lesson showed me how lagging, rolling, and feature selection work together to improve the accuracy, efficiency, and overall effectiveness of machine learning models.
