what_i_learned: |
  Today was our orientation, and it turned out to be both informative and engaging. I had the opportunity to meet my research group for the first time, which helped me become more comfortable with the people I will be working alongside throughout this experience. Getting introduced to everyone and hearing a little about their backgrounds made the environment feel welcoming and collaborative from the start.
    
    During orientation, I was also introduced to Figma and learned about its role in UI and UX design. While I already had some basic familiarity with design concepts, this session went into much greater detail than I had previously experienced. One of the most interesting topics for me was UX, or user experience. Before today, I had not really heard much about UX or fully understood how important it is in the development of applications, websites, and other forms of technology. Learning that UX focuses on how users interact with and feel while using a product gave me a new perspective on design and technology overall. It was interesting to see how even small design choices can affect usability, accessibility, and the overall experience for users.
   
     We also participated in a Bingo-style activity that helped everyone get to know one another better. Through this activity, I learned more about my associates’ personalities, interests, goals, and hobbies. It gave me a more in-depth and personal perspective on their everyday lives outside of academics or work. Hearing about everyone’s ambitions and unique interests made the activity enjoyable and helped build stronger connections within the group. Overall, the orientation was a positive experience that not only introduced me to new concepts like UX design and Figma but also helped me feel more connected to my peers and research team.
