what_i_learned: |
 Today I learned more about Overleaf and how it simplifies the process of creating professional technical documents and research papers. I explored how to insert and format images, adjust their placement, and add captions and labels for proper referencing throughout a document. I also gained a better understanding of organizing sections, citations, and formatting to meet academic publishing standards. Learning these features showed me how Overleaf and LaTeX help produce clean, consistent, and well-structured papers that are commonly used in engineering and computer science research. The collaborative tools also make it easier for multiple authors to edit and review the same document while maintaining version control.

  I also learned about hyperparameters in machine learning and how they influence the performance of machine learning models. Unlike model parameters that are learned during training, hyperparameters are configured before training begins and control how the learning process operates. Examples such as learning rate, batch size, maximum tree depth, and the number of estimators can significantly affect a model's accuracy and ability to generalize to new data. Proper hyperparameter tuning helps reduce overfitting and underfitting while improving the overall stability and efficiency of a model. Understanding these concepts gave me a better appreciation of how careful experimentation and optimization can produce more reliable and accurate machine learning solutions.
