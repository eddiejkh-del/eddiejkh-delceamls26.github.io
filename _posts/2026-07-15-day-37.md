what_i_learned: |
 Today I learned several editing techniques in Adobe Premiere while helping create our 90-second pitch video. One of the most valuable skills I gained was how to incorporate B-roll footage to make the presentation more engaging and visually appealing. Instead of relying only on the primary footage, I learned that B-roll provides additional context, supports the narration, and helps keep the audience interested throughout the video. I also practiced trimming clips, arranging them on the timeline, and creating smooth transitions between scenes to improve the overall flow. Another important lesson was how to synchronize video clips with narration so that the visuals matched what was being explained. These editing techniques helped transform our rough footage into a more polished and professional presentation.

 Working with Adobe Premiere also showed me how much planning and attention to detail goes into producing a short but effective video. Since we only had 90 seconds to communicate our message, every clip and transition had to serve a purpose. Adding B-roll at the right moments made the video feel more dynamic while reinforcing the key points of our presentation. Collaborating with my teammates also made the editing process more efficient because we could share ideas and provide feedback on what worked best. Overall, this experience improved both my technical editing skills and my understanding of how thoughtful video production can make a presentation more impactful and memorable.
