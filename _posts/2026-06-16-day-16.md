what_i_learned: |
  Today I learned more about lagging and rolling techniques in machine learning and how they improve the performance of predictive models. Lagging creates new features using previous values from a dataset, allowing the model to learn patterns and trends from past observations. Rolling techniques use statistics such as rolling averages, sums, or standard deviations over a moving window of data. These methods help preserve important historical information that may not be visible from individual data points alone. By incorporating lagged and rolling features, models can better capture temporal relationships and reduce the impact of short-term noise.

  I also learned how these features can enhance tree-based models such as Decision Trees, Random Forests, and Gradient Boosting models. Since tree-based algorithms cannot automatically recognize time dependencies, lagging and rolling features provide valuable context for making more accurate predictions. They help the model identify recurring patterns, trends, and seasonal behavior within the data. As a result, the model can retain more key information from previous observations and improve its overall predictive effectiveness.
