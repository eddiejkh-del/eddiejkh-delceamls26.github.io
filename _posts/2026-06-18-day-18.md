what_i_learned: |
 Today I learned the difference between chatbots and artificial intelligence in the context of AI Engineering. Chatbots are applications that use AI to communicate with users, while AI itself is a much broader field that includes machine learning, computer vision, and predictive analytics. Understanding this difference helped me see how chatbots are only one practical use of AI technologies. I also learned about feature selection and how it is used to identify the most important variables within a dataset. One technique I studied was Mutual Information, which measures how much useful information a feature provides about the target variable.

  I further learned that feature selection can help stabilize machine learning models by reducing unnecessary or redundant data. This process improves model efficiency and can help prevent overfitting during training. Feature selection is especially useful for tree-based models, regression models, and boosting algorithms because it helps them focus on the most meaningful patterns in the data. By using relevant features, these models can produce more accurate and reliable predictions. Overall, this lesson showed me how proper feature selection plays a critical role in building strong and effective machine learning systems.
