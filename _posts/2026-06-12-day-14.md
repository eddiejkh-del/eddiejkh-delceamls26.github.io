what_i_learned: |
  Today, I learned about the satellites used to monitor Aerosol Optical Depth (AOD), which is an important measurement for assessing the concentration of aerosols in the atmosphere. Understanding how satellite-based observations collect and analyze AOD data helped me gain insight into how researchers monitor air quality, atmospheric conditions, and climate-related processes on a global scale. These satellite systems provide valuable data that can be used to track aerosol distribution, identify pollution sources, and support environmental research. Learning about these technologies helped me better understand the role of remote sensing in atmospheric science and how large-scale datasets are collected for analysis.

  I also learned more about Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) and their role in the formation of secondary organic aerosols. VOCs are emitted from both natural and human-made sources and can undergo chemical reactions in the atmosphere, producing organic aerosols that contribute to air pollution and influence climate and human health. Additionally, I learned about the Planetary Boundary Layer (PBL), which is the lowest part of the troposphere and is directly influenced by the Earth's surface. The PBL plays a critical role in the mixing and transport of heat, moisture, pollutants, and aerosols throughout the atmosphere. Understanding the relationship between VOCs, aerosol formation, and the Planetary Boundary Layer has provided me with a deeper understanding of atmospheric processes and their importance in environmental and climate research.
