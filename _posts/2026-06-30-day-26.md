what_i_learned: |
 Today I learned about several emerging technologies and cybersecurity practices that are shaping the future of artificial intelligence in both government and industry. One of the topics that stood out was Axonius Federal Systems and how it uses AI to automate traceability, continuous compliance, and the management of HTL process artifacts. Instead of relying on one-time audits, the platform creates an automated dashboard that continuously monitors compliance, identifies missing tools or requirements, and helps organizations stay prepared for security assessments. I also learned about the Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC), the Department of Defense framework designed to ensure contractors protect sensitive unclassified information through standardized cybersecurity practices. Another important concept was Red Hat's mission of bridging innovation with institutional security while supporting the dual mandate of advancing U.S. leadership in AI and addressing emerging threats through secure, agentic, and autonomous AI systems.

 I also learned how Red Hat's AI ecosystem uses model servers such as vLLM inference to efficiently distribute large language model workloads across multiple systems, making AI inference faster, more flexible, and more scalable. Their collaboration with NVIDIA demonstrated how organizations can transform raw data into actionable intelligence while accelerating the deployment of agentic AI solutions. Another key takeaway was the importance of AI operations, which focus on observing, tracing, and evaluating AI systems while enforcing strong identity and access control through technologies such as SPIRE. I learned that AI platforms must be continuously monitored, updated, and redeployed to maintain reliability and security, with engineered trust built around platform integrity, model integrity, and outcome integrity. Finally, I learned how MCP servers and gateways provide centralized control over AI agents by managing how they interact with tools and ensuring they can only access approved models that comply with organizational policies.
