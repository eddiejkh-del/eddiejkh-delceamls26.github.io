what_i_learned: |
 Today I began learning about the AdaBoost modeling algorithm and how it is used in machine learning to improve prediction accuracy. AdaBoost, which stands for Adaptive Boosting, is an ensemble learning method that combines multiple weak learners into a stronger and more accurate model. I learned that decision trees with a single split, often called decision stumps, are commonly used as the weak learners in AdaBoost. Instead of relying on one model, AdaBoost trains several models in sequence, with each new model focusing more on the samples that were classified incorrectly by the previous one. This iterative learning process allows the algorithm to gradually reduce prediction errors and improve overall performance. I also learned that AdaBoost is effective for many classification problems because it continuously adapts to difficult training examples.
 
Another concept I learned was how AdaBoost assigns weights to both the training data and the individual models it creates. Samples that are misclassified receive higher weights, encouraging the next weak learner to pay closer attention to those difficult cases. At the same time, models that perform better are given greater influence when making the final prediction. I also discovered that AdaBoost can achieve high accuracy without requiring an overly complex model, making it an efficient choice for many machine learning tasks. Learning about AdaBoost helped me better understand the value of ensemble methods and how combining multiple simple models can produce stronger and more reliable predictions than using a single model alone.
