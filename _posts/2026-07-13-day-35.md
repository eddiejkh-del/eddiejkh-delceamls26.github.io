what_i_learned: |
 Today I learned about the process of movie making by taking on the roles of both the director and script writer while producing a 90-second elevator pitch. Serving as the script writer taught me how important it is to create a clear and engaging story within a very limited amount of time. Every line of dialogue had to contribute to the main message, making word choice and pacing essential. As the director, I learned how to guide the actors, determine camera angles, and ensure that each scene effectively communicated the intended idea. I also discovered that directing requires balancing creativity with organization so that filming stays efficient and on schedule. Throughout the production process, I gained a greater appreciation for how much planning is required before the camera even begins recording. Storyboarding, rehearsing scenes, and coordinating each person's responsibilities were all critical to creating a polished final product. This experience demonstrated that successful filmmaking depends on both technical preparation and creative storytelling working together.

  Working on the elevator pitch also strengthened my communication, leadership, and teamwork skills. Because the presentation was limited to only 90 seconds, every scene had to capture the audience's attention while delivering information quickly and effectively. I learned how directors must make decisions that keep the production moving while ensuring everyone understands their role. Revising the script several times showed me the importance of editing and removing unnecessary details to create a stronger final message. Collaborating with the actors allowed me to see how different performances can influence the tone and effectiveness of a scene. I also realized that filming often requires multiple takes and adjustments before achieving the desired result. Overall, this project gave me hands-on experience with the filmmaking process and helped me better understand the responsibilities involved in directing and script writing. It also showed me that even a short 90-second video requires careful planning, creativity, and teamwork to communicate a message successfully.
