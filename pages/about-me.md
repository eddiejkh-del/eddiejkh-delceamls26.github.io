    Hey! I'm Eddie King-Hedgspeth, an electrical engineer with a concentration in computer architecture, and a  student in the CEAMLS SAIRI Summer Research program.

     My research interests lie at the intersection of human-computer interaction (HCI) and computer engineering, with a particular focus on the hardware systems that enable modern computing technologies. I am especially interested in the design and development of microelectronics, semiconductor devices, and computer chips that power interactive systems and intelligent applications. By exploring how advancements in chip architecture, embedded systems, and hardware optimization influence the performance, efficiency, and usability of technology, I hope to contribute to the creation of faster, more reliable, and more user-centered computing solutions. This combination of HCI and microelectronics allows me to examine both the technical foundations of computing and the ways people interact with and benefit from these innovations.
